One of the most common questions we get from prospective partners isn't "does estate planning make sense for my business" — most people arrive already convinced of that. It's "which model should I actually run?"

Spark Solutions offers three ways in: White-Label Partner, Branded Consultant, and Referrer/Introducer. Each is a genuinely different business decision, not just a pricing tier, so it's worth working through which one actually fits your business rather than defaulting to whichever sounds most impressive.

## The three models, briefly

**White-Label Partner.** A fully rebranded platform — your logo, your domain, your brand throughout the entire client journey. Clients never see the Spark Solutions name. You connect your own Stripe account and take payment directly; Spark Solutions charges a flat monthly platform fee.

**Branded Consultant.** The platform runs under the Spark Solutions brand rather than yours. You still onboard clients and earn from the work, but with a faster setup and lower ongoing cost, in exchange for less control over presentation.

**Referrer/Introducer.** You make the introduction and step back. The client is handled entirely through Spark Solutions' branded journey, and you earn a commission on completed business. There's no platform to configure and no ongoing service delivery on your side at all.

## Comparing them properly

### White-Label Partner

**Best for:** established firms with a recognisable brand and existing client trust — mortgage brokerages, IFA practices, accountancy firms — where clients would find it odd to be redirected to an unfamiliar name mid-relationship.

**Pros:**
- Full brand control — the client experience feels like a natural extension of your existing business, not a hand-off.
- You take payment directly via your own Stripe account, which typically means the strongest margin per client of the three models.
- Strengthens client retention, since the estate planning relationship stays visibly attached to your firm.

**Cons:**
- Requires the most setup: branding assets, domain configuration, and getting your team familiar with the client wizard.
- Ongoing platform fee applies regardless of volume, so it works best once you have reasonable, consistent client flow to justify it.

**Revenue implications:** highest revenue per client, because you're not sharing margin with a referral commission structure — you're paying a fixed fee for the infrastructure and keeping the rest.

### Branded Consultant

**Best for:** firms that want to start earning from estate planning quickly, without the setup overhead of a full rebrand — or that want to trial the model before committing to White-Label.

**Pros:**
- Fastest of the two "active delivery" models to get live.
- Lower ongoing cost than White-Label, since there's no custom branding to build or maintain.
- Still lets you actively manage the client relationship and earn accordingly, unlike a pure referral.

**Cons:**
- Clients see the Spark Solutions brand, not yours, which some established firms find inconsistent with their positioning.
- Less differentiation if a competitor in your area also runs as a Branded Consultant.

**Revenue implications:** a solid middle ground — meaningfully better margins than pure referral, at a fraction of the setup effort of White-Label.

### Referrer/Introducer

**Best for:** businesses that come across estate planning need naturally but don't want to run any part of the service themselves — smaller practices, accountants focused purely on their core discipline, or anyone testing whether their client base actually wants this before investing further.

**Pros:**
- Zero setup. There's genuinely nothing to configure.
- No ongoing platform fee or commitment.
- Lowest-risk way to find out whether your clients respond to the offer at all.

**Cons:**
- Lowest revenue per client of the three models, since you're earning commission rather than the full fee.
- No brand presence in the client journey, so it does little to reinforce your own firm's positioning.
- Less control over timing and follow-up, since the process is run entirely by Spark Solutions.

**Revenue implications:** meaningful, genuinely passive income with no delivery cost — but the lowest ceiling of the three.

## A simple way to decide

Ask yourself three questions:

- **Do my clients already strongly identify with my brand?** If yes, White-Label protects and extends that. If your relationship is more transactional, brand control matters less.
- **How much time can I invest in setup this month?** If the honest answer is "almost none," start as a Referrer or Branded Consultant and upgrade later — nothing about starting there locks you out of White-Label down the line.
- **What's my client volume?** A handful of estate planning conversations a year rarely justifies a monthly platform fee; a strong, consistent flow usually does, and quickly pays for itself given the fee structure detailed on our [pricing page](/pricing).

Many partners don't pick once and stay there forever — a common path is starting as a Referrer to validate demand, moving to Branded Consultant once conversion proves out, then adopting White-Label once volume justifies full brand control.
